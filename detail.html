NDSM: graffiti art.

The NDSM neighborhood is a lively area. You will find countless murals here, as well as artists' homes and studios. The NDSM wharf has been a huge photogenic hotspot for a number of years, the number of bars and restaurants is still growing. The site is more than ten football fields in size, and therefore offers plenty of space for many activities for young and old.

How to get there

There are two free ferries, which are accessible for pedestrians and (moped) cyclists to the NSDM wharf The ferry departs from Amsterdam Central every 15 minutes during rush hours, outside rush hours every half hour. Sailing time is approximately 15 minutes. From the Pontsteiger you can go to the NDSM wharf in less than ten minutes.

Impressive murals and street art

NDSM is one of the best places for graffiti and street art in the city. The STREET Museum is a great place to start if you want to experience urban art. More than 150 works can be admired here, some of which are huge and reach the ceiling.

What else to do in NDSM

The lively social area hides a wealth of activities. Scattered among the shipping containers and hangars, you'll find eco-friendly waterfront restaurants and beach bars to relax in. The neighborhood is home to one of the largest flea markets in Europe and an open-air cinema. If you don't just want to see canals and tulips in Amsterdam, NDSM is a great place to be.

Flea market IJ-hallen

The NDSM neighborhood is a mecca for lovers of vintage and antique goods. You can spend hours wandering the IJ-Hallen flea market hunting for vintage treasures. IJ-hallen is the largest flea market in Europe. Due to the size of the market, the chances of success are very high. Unique products are offered at very low prices. Hundreds of stalls offer everything from clothing and jewelry to kitchen supplies and home furnishings. It's a great place to look for second-hand, upcycled items.

Festival vibes

The NDSM wharf provides space for many festivals throughout the year. The festivals are focused on different music genres and celebrations. For those who like a bit of funk, for example, the Wicked Jazz Sounds Festival is interesting. At 'De Zon' you can go for House, Disco, Latin, Party and Dutch music. The NDSM Food Festival in August is a must for culinary delights. Enjoy snacks and drinks as the city's food trucks travel to NDSM for a lovely long weekend. With snacks, beers, music and children's entertainment, there really is something for everyone
